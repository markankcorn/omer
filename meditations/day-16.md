Sometimes the truly compassionate thing to do is to hold back. I see this from time to time with my kids. I want to keep all manner of harm away from them, any tiny scrape or scratch or hurt feelings or disappointment. Like all caring parents.

But ultimately there is, to quote eminent child psychologist Wendy Mogel, a blessing in a skinned knee. Trying and failing and learning that it doesn't break you and getting back up again to try once more, that's the deeper sense of love and compassion that we can give our children.

Think of ways today that holding back our compassion might just be the best thing for the recipient. And possibly ourselves — maybe sometimes we need that bucket of cold water to motivate us or to teach us a more lasting lesson.
